Misclick Rate Results

  • A one-tailed t-test is appropriate to use when one expects one group to be greater or lesser than the other. For this metric, we opted for a one-tail t-test because I hypothesize the misclick rate of Version A to be greater than Version B.
  • P-value represents the probability of observing if the null hypothesis is true. A p-value less than the significance level (typically 0.05) means you reject the null hypothesisis. The p-value for a t-statistic of 2.924988129 for a one-tailed test is 0.00309 (1 - 0.9969066735). The p-value is less than 0.05 so we reject the null hypothesis and have found statistically significant evidence that the alternative hypothesis is true.
  • Other important findings that were revealed from this analysis can be seen through the averages of both verions where Verion A had an average misclick rate of 0.2058823529 whereas Version's B was 0. This tells us there were no misclicks on Version B but some on Version A.
  • We reject the null hypothesis.
